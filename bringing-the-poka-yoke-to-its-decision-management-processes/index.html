Bringing the poka-yoke to IT’s decision management processes

In lean processes, there’s a concept called the poka-yoke which is the deliberate design to apply some mistake proofing, and promote correct use in all aspects.

In process design, this is similar to matching meeting agenda items to links on a decision board, by making the process completely transparent from multiple views, and by empowering individuals, and stakeholders as a way to encourage adoption and involvement.

Opus Icon looks for opportunities to apply the poka-yoke at every turn.

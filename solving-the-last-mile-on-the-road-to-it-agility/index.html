Solving the last mile on the road to IT agility

The last mile is IT

A while ago our team got in a discussion about choosing Kanban or Scrum. We even had a meeting about considering a new method. At the end of that meeting we had come to 2 decisions.

  1. That switching from Scrum to Kan-ban wouldn’t address the core challenges of being more agile and pivoting as fast as the business wanted.
  2. The root cause limiting TechOps and DevOps ability to pivot when needed is we were in the unfortunate position of making trade-off decisions on behalf of our primary stake holders. This took, not only time, but

IT is much different than any other department. The decision to centralized resources to manage people responsible for information technology has only been around for the last 50 years, while centralized accounting, sales and operations have been around since before capitalism. And the entire company is on the customer list, and every project or goal is a potential source of work/value stream to consider for scheduling.

Kanban and scrum assume there’s one product owner, while IT’s customer list changes almost as much as the volume of work needed for each customer backlog. We need a tool to make prioritization easier.

The secret to doing IT great is losing your ego and really understanding the problem at a systemic level. Only then will you have a crisp target for your solution.

The underlying cause for the lack of agility was that when IT was asked to “prioritize” a thing either already on the schedule, or not even estimated, IT was standing in and making trade-off decisions on behalf of most of the stakeholders.

Understand the why before you get to how

We needed a way to distribute the trade off decisions to the right people, and in most escalation exercises, IT was usually there to facilitate, and own any issues. We determined that IT could facilitate a discussion between two product or service owners to make the trade off decision process faster. It could even be beneficial to have someone representing IT in the discussion to ensure clarity and perspective.

Budgeting with points instead of dollar$

In a sprint planning meeting someone mentioned the concept of SRE and that it uses a budgeting model, and after a little white-boarding, we found that we can apply a lightweight financial budgeting model to story points.

IT knows its total capacity in points, and can easily adjust or reallocate points as they wish. The only catch is that points have a limited lifespan, and get reissued every sprint or schedule cycle.

Now Picture IT

Imagine if you had IOU’s for IT work that magically appear on your desk. Now those IOU’s are only good for 2 weeks, and you’re aloud to use them as you wish. Spend them, trade them for favors, floating around the office, and can easily be tracked on the Team Dashboard by Department or other stakeholder team.
